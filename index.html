OUR HISTORY



Over the last twenty years in Kenya, InCA has acquired a client base in virtually every economic activity. These include Government agencies, private sector clients in ICT and fast moving consumer goods, NGOs, the UN, the World Bank and international broadcasters. Whether we are filming, designing or consulting, we thus handle an exceptionally wide variety of subjects including finance, information technology, agriculture, conservation, healthcare, consumer research and human rights.

Our Advantage

InCA's founding members have been professionally active for more than thirty years in their respective fields.

We have made it our business to expose ourselves continuously to new developments in all provinces of our business, from television production, technology to management ideas.

International Exposure

Our experience has taken us well beyond the borders of Kenya. We have filmed in more than fourteen African countries, as well as Europe, the United States, Japan, India and the Arctic.

Our human resource clients are also widely dispersed, including clients from the US as well as the African continent.

Diverse Client Base

Over the last twenty years in Kenya, InCA has acquired a client base in virtually every sphere of economic activity.

These include: Government agencies, Private sector clients, Insurance, ICT, FMCGs, NGOs and International Agencies, including the UN and the World Bank.

RICHARD VAUGHAN
Managing Director

Richard Vaughan has been a producer and director of factual films for more than thirty years. Originally trained at the BBC in London, he has worked in fourteen African countries, as well as Europe, the United States, Japan, India, Israel and Greenland. His films have been broadcast in more than a hundred countries around the world, and he has won awards from the British Medical Association, the Royal Television Society, Semana Internacional de Cine Cientifico (Spain) and ITB Berlin.

He has also written a number of books on the social economy of African countries, and consults on creative thinking. He is a graduate with First Class Honors in Social Science from the University of Bristol, UK (1979).

JERUSHA AROTHE-VAUGHAN
HR Manager

Jerusha Arothe-Vaughan is a specialist in human resource development and management, particularly as they apply on the African continent. She is highly experienced in assessing human resource needs and identifying the solutions to address them, particularly in the design of training programmes. Other fields of expertise include strategic planning, salary and benefits surveys, employee opinion surveys, job evaluation, grading structures and performance management systems.

Over the last twenty years, her client base has comprised the United Nations and other international NGOs, numerous multinational corporations with an interest in Africa, and a wide variety of banks, insurance companies and other financial institutions within East Africa. She has also consulted for NGOs and foundations in the United States.

She has an Honours Graduate in English Literature and Education from the University of Makerere (1971).

PATRICK WAHOME
IT Specialist

Patrick Wahome specializes in information technology as it relates to graphic design and publishing. With twenty years’ experience, he has undertaken branding work for numerous high profile clients throughout East Africa, including banks, insurance companies, NGOs, government institutions, investment and tourism promotion bodies. He has been responsible for a wide variety of annual reports and corporate publications, notably for Government institutions and the
World Bank.

FILM



InCA was founded in 1985 by a group television producers trained at the BBC in London. Over the last thirty years we have won many awards, particularly when our programmes have been dealing with scientific, industrial or medical issues. InCA has worked extensively for prestigious science television strands, including Horizon for the BBC, NOVA for PBS and Quantum for ABC in Australia.

Subjects our Kenyan office has handled include reproductive health, biotechnology, healthcare for children in Africa, complementary medicine, wildlife management, circumcision, innovative farming techniques, religious cults, drug abuse, African rock art and forest conservation.

Many of our locally broadcast documentaries have had a major impact on public opinion in Kenya, particularly when dealing with issues pertaining to human rights. Some have been instrumental in amending Parliamentary legislation in favour of the poor and powerless.

We have also serviced a variety of government agencies and parastatals in Uganda, promoting the country's tourism potential in particular.

MANAGEMENT CONSULTANCY



Since its foundation in 1996, the Management Consultancy department has serviced a wide variety of clients from multinational companies to modestly funded NGOs. Without exception, they have been struck by an approach that discards outdated and parochial management styles, replacing them with the more open, less formal approach that is essential to competition in the global economy. Unlike most management consultancies, we do not see our service simply as an extension of the auditing function: we are first and foremost specialists in human resource development.

Our clients have included Kenya Commercial Bank, KK Security, CFC Life Insurance Company, Citibank, Unilever, BAT, Cadbury (K) Ltd, UK AID, the Canadian International Development Agency and several UN Agencies. In the United States, we have worked for the International Women’s Media Foundation and Encompass.


All our courses require a proactive approach from participants, based frequently on their own experiences.

Exercises and group work form a key component of our training activities.
